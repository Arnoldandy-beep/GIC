2 · Recording a Savings Deposit

ℹ️
The standard monthly contribution is UGX 100,000. Partial or double payments are allowed — use the Notes field to flag them.
  • 1
    Go to Savings in the top navigation bar.
  • 2
    Click the + Add Savings button (top-right of the page).
  • 3
    Select the Member from the dropdown.
  • 4
    Enter the Amount (defaults to 100,000).
  • 5
    Set the Date Deposited (defaults to today). Always use the actual receipt date.
  • 6
    Fill in Notes — e.g. "Monthly contribution", "Partial payment", "Double contribution".
  • 7
    Click 💾 Record & Return. The entry saves immediately.
⚠️
A savings entry posted by a non-treasurer user goes into a Pending queue and requires your approval. As Treasurer you can approve or reject those from the Savings page.
✏️

3 · Correcting or Voiding a Savings Entry (Amendment)

Use this when you need to fix a wrong amount or date, or mark a duplicate entry as void. Amendments are maker-checker: if you are the maker, a second admin (IT Admin) must approve. If the IT Admin is the maker, you (Treasurer) can be the approver.

  • 1
    Go to Savings and find the entry to correct.
  • 2
    Click the ✏️ Amend action button on that row.
  • 3
    Choose Correct Amount (enter the right figure and date) or Void Entry (marks it cancelled).
  • 4
    Enter a clear Reason — this is audited.
  • 5
    Click Submit for Approval →. The request is sent to IT Admin for confirmation.
✅
The original entry stays unchanged until the approver confirms. An approved amendment updates the savings tally and timeline automatically.
🗑️

4 · Deleting a Savings Entry

Treasurers (and IT Admin) can delete savings records directly. Other users only raise a deletion request that requires approval.

  • 1
    Go to Savings, find the entry.
  • 2
    Click the 🗑️ Delete button on that row.
  • 3
    Confirm the warning prompt. The record is removed immediately.
🚨
Irreversible. Prefer the Void amendment path above unless the entry is a clear error that must not appear in history at all.

5 · Creating a New Loan

⚠️
Paper first, system second. Only open a loan in the system once you have verified a signed paper loan application form. The loan stays in Pending status until 3 of the 4 committee approvers confirm.
For a Club Member
  • 1
    Go to Loans → click + New Loan.
  • 2
    Leave the type as 🏅 Club Member (5%/mo).
  • 3
    Select the Borrower from the dropdown — it shows each member's available savings next to their name.
  • 4
    Enter Amount, confirm Period (1–6 months) and Date Issued.
  • 5
    The live calculator shows: Monthly Instalment, Total Interest and Total Repayable. Confirm these match the paper form.
  • 6
    Fill Purpose.
  • 7
    Select Guarantor 1 and Guarantor 2. The system checks their available savings covers the total repayable — a warning appears if they fall short.
  • 8
    Click Submit Loan. Status becomes Pending.
For a Non-Member
  • 1
    Select 👤 Non-Member (10%/mo). The borrower dropdown hides and a free-text Borrower Full Name field appears.
  • 2
    Fill in Collateral description and Estimated Value.
  • 3
    Still requires two club-member guarantors. Continue from step 7 above.
Recording a Legacy / Historical Loan
  • 1
    Set the Date Issued to the actual historical date (before 5 Apr 2026). The form automatically enables Legacy Capture Mode.
  • 2
    Fill in Date Approved, Date Disbursed, Date Paid Off (if already cleared), Historical Status, and Approvers on Record (comma-separated names).
  • 3
    Submit. Legacy loans can be corrected later via Edit Legacy Loan.
ℹ️
After submitting, open the loan detail, scroll to Documentation and click 📎 Upload scanned loan form to attach the signed paper form image or PDF.
✅

6 · Approving a Loan

Loans require 3 out of 4 approvals from: IT Admin, Chairman, Treasurer, Secretary. Any eligible member can approve from the loan detail view.

  • 1
    Go to Loans → click the Pending tab.
  • 2
    Click the loan row to open its detail.
  • 3
    Click ✅ Approve. Once 3 approvals are collected, the loan status changes to Active automatically.
  • 4
    To reject, click ❌ Decline and enter a reason.
⚠️
Record the two manual signatories in the Signatory 1 / Signatory 2 fields on the loan detail for audit trail purposes.

7 · Recording a Loan Repayment

ℹ️
Every installment settles outstanding interest first, then principal (standard posting rule). The system does this automatically based on the Payment Type you select.
  • 1
    Go to Loans → click the Active tab.
  • 2
    Click the loan row to open it, then click + Payment (top-right of the detail view).
  • 3
    The modal shows outstanding balances — Total Outstanding, Interest Outstanding, Principal Outstanding, and the Standard Instalment amount.
  • 4
    Choose Payment Type:
    • Installment — normal monthly payment; interest cleared first, then principal.
    • Interest Only — member is paying interest portion alone this month (does not reduce principal).
  • 5
    Enter the Amount received (defaults to the smaller of standard instalment or outstanding balance).
  • 6
    Set the Payment Date and add Notes (e.g. "Mobile Money 0703-XXXXXX", "Cash").
  • 7
    Click 💾 Record.
⚠️
If the loan is overdue, the modal will display the extra penalty interest already added. Collect and record that too before closing the loan.

8 · Closing a Loan (Auto-Close)

There is no manual "close loan" button. The system marks a loan Cleared automatically the moment its outstanding balance reaches zero.

  • 1
    Record repayments as described in Step 7 above, one installment at a time (or a lump-sum if the borrower wants to pay off everything).
  • 2
    On the last repayment, enter the exact remaining outstanding amount. The system will detect the balance hits zero and flip the loan to Cleared.
  • 3
    After closure, the guarantors' locked savings are released automatically.
  • 4
    If required, remove the uploaded loan form document by clicking 🗑️ Remove uploaded form (only visible on Cleared loans).
✅
Check: the repayment progress ring in the loan detail should show 100% and the status badge should read Cleared.
⚠️

10 · Recording a Club Expense

ℹ️
Expenses draw from operational funds (annual fees + other income), never from the savings pool or loan interest. The available operational balance is shown in the form.
  • 1
    Go to Expenses → click + Record Expense.
  • 2
    Enter Amount, Date, Purpose / Description, and Category.
  • 3
    Select the Approver — choose Chairman, Secretary, or IT Admin (never yourself).
  • 4
    Add any Supporting Notes (receipt number, context).
  • 5
    Click Submit for Approval. The selected approver sees it in their Expenses view and can confirm or reject.

To log other income (donations, penalties received as cash), click + Record Operational Income on the same page and fill in the source and amount.

13 · Year-End Interest Distribution

🚨
Run once per year on 31 December only. This action distributes the annual interest pool proportionally to members' savings balances. It cannot be rolled back easily.
  • 1
    Go to Reports → click 📊 Interest Distribution.
  • 2
    Review the preview table: Interest Pool, Effective Rate, and each member's share. Cross-check total against bank records.
  • 3
    If figures look correct, click ✅ Process. The interest is posted as savings credits for every eligible member.

14 · Running the Member Data Audit

The audit report gives a quick data integrity check — savings totals, locked savings, outstanding loans, and any orphan records.

  • 1
    Go to Reports → click 🧮 Member Data Audit.
  • 2
    The header row shows: total members, combined savings + locked, active loan exposure + overdue count.
  • 3
    Any red alert banners at the top flag orphan data (savings or loans with no matching member).
  • 4
    The per-member table lists savings, locked amount, available savings, active borrowing, guarantees, and outstanding balance. A ⚠ Issue badge highlights anomalies.
  • 5
    Investigate flagged rows and reconcile via the relevant savings or loan records. Contact IT Admin for database-level fixes.

15 · Offboarding a Member

⚠️
Only offboard a member after all their active loans are fully cleared and the committee has approved the withdrawal. An exit fee of 5% of total savings is deducted.
  • 1
    Go to Members → open the member's profile.
  • 2
    Click 🚪 Offboard. The form shows the Total Savings, the 5% Exit Fee, and the Net Payable figure to the member.
  • 3
    Enter Exit Date, Reason, and Notes / Witnesses.
  • 4
    Click 🚪 Confirm Offboard. The member status changes to Exited.

Common Mistakes to Avoid
  • Recording a loan before the paper form is signed — always paper first.
  • Using Interest Only mode when the member is paying a full installment — this leaves principal untouched.
  • Running the year-end interest distribution before Dec 31 — preview it, but do not press Process until the correct date.
  • Deleting a savings entry instead of voiding it — deletion removes all audit history.
  • Selecting yourself as the expense approver — the form prevents this but be mindful.
Loan Diagnostics (Treasurer-Only View)

On any loan detail page, a Loan Diagnostics card appears for Treasurer and IT Admin only. It shows the Effective Due Date, Late Days (calendar days after due date), and Penalty Tier (penalty increases every 30 late days). Use this to explain to a borrower exactly how much penalty they owe.
